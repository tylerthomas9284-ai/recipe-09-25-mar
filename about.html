Our Culinary
Philosophy

We believe cooking is an act of design. Every dish is a composition. Every technique is a system. Every flavor is intentional. We built Flavor & Form to prove that constraint breeds creativity — that strict principles don't limit great food, they define it.

Origin Story

Where It Began

In 2019, chef Adriana Cruz returned from three years working in Michelin-starred kitchens in Copenhagen and Barcelona with a single conviction: the principles that govern great modernist design — geometric rigor, form following function, and ruthless elimination of the unnecessary — were precisely the principles that governed great cooking.

She founded Flavor & Form in a 400-square-foot test kitchen in Washington's Columbia Heights neighborhood with $12,000 in savings, a KitchenAid mixer, and a blog. Within eight months, the site had 200,000 monthly readers. The brown butter cookie recipe we published in March 2023 crashed our servers twice.

Today, Flavor & Form reaches 1.2 million monthly readers across the United States, publishes 3 new tested recipes per week, and offers a membership program serving over 12,000 home cooks and culinary professionals.

Form Follows
Flavor.

The Bauhaus school taught us that objects stripped of ornament — built purely for purpose — become more beautiful than anything decorated could ever be. We apply this to our cooking.

Remove everything unnecessary from a dish. What remains is not sparse — it is essential. Each ingredient earns its place. Each technique has a function. Each plating choice communicates something precise about what you're about to taste.

This is not austere cooking. This is cooking that has been thought through so thoroughly that every single element works — visually, texturally, and on the palate.

01

Precision First

Every recipe is tested a minimum of six times. Measurements are exact. Temperatures are verified. Timing windows are validated across different oven types. We don't guess. We measure.

02

Technique Over Trend

Trends cycle. Technique endures. Our curriculum is built on foundational methods — mother sauces, knife skills, bread fermentation, sauce reduction — that will be relevant in 50 years.

03

Beauty Through Restraint

A dish does not need ten components to be beautiful. It needs the right two or three, placed with intention. Our plating philosophy draws directly from Bauhaus geometric composition principles.
